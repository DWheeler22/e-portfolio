About Me

Background

Hi, I'm DJ Wheeler, a Cybersecurity major at Penn State. I was born in Fauquier County, Virginia, but moved to the small town of Monroe, Connecticut where I was raised into adulthood. From a very young age, I was fascinated by computers and technology, and my family would often refer to me to solve technical problems. This early exposure steered me towards the Penn State College of Information Sciences and Technology. Since joining this college, I found a passion for cybersecurity, particularly in ethical hacking and network defense. I enjoy learning about operating systems, networking protocols, and vulnerability assessment tools.

Involvement

At Penn State, I am actively involved in the Competitive Cybersecurity Organization (CCSO), where I am a practicing member of the Red Team. In this role, I practice offensive security skills several times a week to practice for competitions. This includes completing offensive security labs through platforms like TryHackMe and HackTheBox. I gain experience in a team environment by collaborating with other team members to penetration test cybersecurity ranges. I also use these skills during in house wargaming exercises to prepare the Blue Team for their competitions. Finally, I serve as a mentor to younger students in the club by assisting them in completing introductory red team workshops and labs.

Future Goals

In the future, I aim to further develop my skills in ethical hacking and network defense, with the goal of becoming a certified cybersecurity professional. I plan to pursue certifications such as CompTIA Security+, CEH (Certified Ethical Hacker), OSCP (Offensive Security Certified Professional) to validate my expertise. Additionally, I hope to contribute to the cybersecurity community and eventually work in a role that allows me to protect organizations from cyber threats.
